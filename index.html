Education Hub

Learn about deepfakes, AI-generated media, and how to protect yourself

What Are Deepfakes?

Deepfakes are synthetic media created using artificial intelligence — particularly deep learning algorithms — that manipulate or generate images, videos, and audio to portray real people doing or saying things they never did. The term combines "deep learning" and "fake."

🎭 How They're Made
• GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks)
• Diffusion models (Stable Diffusion, Midjourney)
• Face-swap algorithms (DeepFaceLab)
• Text-to-image AI (DALL-E, Firefly, Grok)
👁️ How to Spot Them
• Unnatural eye blinking or gaze
• Distorted hands or extra fingers
• Blurry edges around face/hair
• Inconsistent lighting on face
Pakistan Context
🗳️ Election Misinformation
During Pakistan's 2024 general elections, multiple deepfake videos and AI-generated images of political leaders circulated on WhatsApp and X/Twitter, falsely showing them making statements they never made. These were used to manipulate voter opinion just days before polling, making digital literacy a matter of democratic importance.
📱 WhatsApp Fake News Crisis
Pakistan has one of the highest WhatsApp usage rates in Asia with over 50 million users. Fake images of accidents, protests, floods, and political events frequently go viral before any verification, causing mass panic and public unrest. Research shows most forwarded content is shared without any fact-checking.
🎙️ Audio Deepfakes in Pakistani Media
Pakistani news cycles have been disrupted by AI-cloned voices of politicians and public figures. Fake audio clips claiming to be leaked private conversations have been used for blackmail and political attacks. The technology to clone a voice now requires as little as 3 seconds of sample audio.
⚖️ Legal Situation in Pakistan
Pakistan's PECA (Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act) 2016 criminalizes online defamation and the spread of fake news, with penalties up to 3 years imprisonment. The FIA Cybercrime Wing handles complaints. However, deepfake-specific legislation is still being drafted and enforcement remains limited due to technical capacity gaps.
How to Protect Yourself Online
🔍

Reverse Image Search

Right-click any suspicious image → "Search image on Google" to find the original source and date

📅

Check the Date & Context

Old images are often reshared out of context. Verify when and where the image was first published

🏛️

Trust Verified Sources

For Pakistan news, verify at Dawn, Geo, ARY, BBC Urdu, or official government portals first

👁️

Inspect Hands & Eyes

AI often gets hands wrong (extra fingers) and eyes look glassy, asymmetric, or have strange reflections

⏸️

Pause Before Sharing

Ask: Is this from a source I trust? Does this seem too outrageous? If in doubt — don't share it out

🔧

Use Detection Tools

Tools like NeuralEye, Google Fact Check, and InVID browser extension can verify suspicious content
